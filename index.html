KARSH ALUMNI AND VISITOR Center

WELCOME HOME, BLUE DEVILS!

Never before have Duke alumni had a place to call their own on campus. Now it’s here—the new Karsh Alumni and Visitors Center. Located at the gateway of campus, at the corner of Chapel Drive and Duke University Road, the new center is the first stop for alumni like you returning to campus. Start your journey here. Get tips for planning your day. Relax with old and new friends in our lobby. Reconnect with the Duke of your past and the Duke of today.

This is your Duke “living room,” and we’re excited to welcome you home.

VIRTUAL TOUR

9 THINGS TO DO AT KARSH

What’s an Alumni Center Good for, Anyway?

  1. Plan your day

    Your first stop on campus! Start at the front desk to plan your day and get tips on where to go and what to see.

  2. Hang out

    Meet up with old friends (and make some new ones).

  3. Grab a Snack

    Visit the Roth Family Market (Coming soon in mid-September!)

  4. Learn new things

    Learn something new about Duke history, notable alumni and campus happenings on our touch-screen digital wall.

  5. reunions central

    Beginning in 2020, Reunions Weekend for all undergraduate alumni will be hosted at the center.

  6. Stretch out

    Break up a day of walking around campus by relaxing on one of the comfy couches or chairs in the atrium. This is your Duke living room, after all.

  7. Visit the library

    See books written by alumni and find your photo in an old Chanticleer yearbook in the Howard Family Library, located in Forlines House.

  8. People-watch

    You never know who will drop by, maybe even a few famous Blue Devils!

  9. Go outside

    Explore the center’s outdoor spaces: Cartwheeling in the quad or walking through the arcades? It’s up to you!

DID YOU KNOW?

The outdoor arcades are made from reclaimed wood from Duke Forest.

What is "the wall?"

Located within our technology hall, the 8-foot digital wall allows visitors to learn about campus happenings, Duke history, who else is on campus and more.

The fireplace in the Moyle Boardroom is made of Duke stone, a signature feature of many buildings on campus including the Duke Chapel.

Who designed the historic home now known as the Forlines House?

Julian Abele, a prominent African-American architect and chief designer of West Campus. Many of the finishes in the renovated building include original wood floors, fireplaces and moldings.

Plants and flowers from the Sarah P. Duke Gardens are a key feature of the center's landscaping.

When was the Duke yearbook, the Chanticleer, first published?

1912. The full collection is on display in the new Howard Family Library, located in Forlines House. Also see a collection of books written by alumni while you're there.

These acoustic panels, along with many other details throughout the center, are inspired by the Gothic architecture of campus.

Déjà vu?

The grassy, outdoor quad located in the center of the alumni center complex is designed to mimic Duke’s West campus quad.

TIMELINE

  • 2009
    The First Home for Alumni Affairs
    In 2009, the Office of Alumni Affairs, then located at 614 Chapel Drive and across from the new Karsh Alumni and Visitors Center, was named the “Forlines House” in honor of John Forlines '39, former university trustee and DAA president. Alumni Affairs staff worked in the original Forlines House up until 2016, when they relocated for maintenance of the building and later to prepare for the construction of the new alumni center.
  • 2016
    A Visit to Stanford
    On a trip to Stanford with the Duke Board of Trustees, philanthropist and former board chairman David Rubenstein '70 saw the Stanford alumni center from the touring bus the trustees contingent was on and immediately asked the bus to stop. “Now this is what Duke needs!” he said, so the story goes, and the idea for the Duke alumni center was born.
  • 2017
    Donors Rally
    The first gift is made to the Duke alumni center…need details here. Over the course of the next two years, more than a dozen alumni would contribute $X.X million toward the construction of the center.
  • March 2017
    Ground Breaks
    The new alumni center starts to take shape at the site at the corner of Duke University Road and Chapel Drive—the gateway to Duke’s iconic campus. Plans for the center include a main public events building with a lobby and a market for snacks and beverages, an Alumni Affairs office building and an administrative building housed in the original, historic faculty home of former Duke dean of Trinity College William H. Wannamaker. The former faculty home later would be renamed “Forlines House,” in honor of John A. Forlines Jr. ’39, who served as Duke Alumni Association president in 1970-71 and whose family provided funding to restore DAA’s previous offices at 614 Chapel Drive as well as the new Forlines House.
  • XXXXX 2018
    Distinctly Duke
    The center exteriors and interiors are designed with a “distinctly Duke” theme in mind. grassy quad in the middle of the center mimics the quad of West campus. A nod to Duke’s gothic architecture can be found in subtle places throughout the center. Reclaimed wood from Duke forest was used throughout the building.
  • September 2019
    Welcome Home
    The new Karsh Alumni and Visitors Center opens to the public with a grand opening celebration on Founders’ Weekend, Sept. 26-28.
